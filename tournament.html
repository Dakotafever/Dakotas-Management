The Rulebook for Devil’s Roulette League Tournaments (Made By Rex!)

Written by: Rex!

The Devil’s Tournament rules are made for competitive purposes and are not restricted to in-game rules. These are meant for community events and competitive sessions. Rules may vary between groups.

Table of Contents

  • 1. Before Playing
  • 2. Playspace
  • 3. Items
  • 4. Coaches and Referee
  • 5. Callouts

1. Before Playing

Players must follow and understand these instructions (only for actual competitive games).

  • Voice chat must be disabled in-game. Discord shall be used for all sessions.
  • Playspace must be properly centered, and players are required to stand during sessions.
  • Maintain respect among all players — no disrespectful gestures allowed.
  • The Ski Mask skin is banned due to no visible pupils, which makes slo-mo effect undetectable.

2. Playspace

All rules apply and are enforced in all games. Referees will issue strikes for rule violations. Two strikes lead to disqualification.

a. Out of Bounds

  • Moving outside of your chair or into walls is illegal.
  • Standing too far back from your chair to avoid bullets is not allowed.
  • Leaning too far forward or crossing the center line is illegal.

b. Avoiding Bullets

  • You may only move when reacting to a bullet (especially in slo-mo).
  • No ducking under the table or raising your position to dodge shots.

3. Items

All rules apply to their respective categories. Standard item rules apply to all maps unless stated otherwise.

a. Standard

  • Do not grab-protect items if another player has a grabber tool.
  • Items must remain visible on the table to avoid cheating.
  • No teaming via item use, except for Med Vote situations.

b. Saloon

  • Bells may not be used more than once per turn (except with a live round shot).
  • Wall bells can only be shot once per magazine.

c. Casino

  • Only one gold item may be used per round.
  • Using a gold taser during someone else’s turn removes the chance to use another gold item until it cycles back.

4. Coaches and Referee

These rules apply only to coaches and referees — not players.

a. Communication

  • Coaches may communicate suggestions to their players.
  • Coaches are responsible for relaying information without exploiting loopholes.
  • They may report unnoticed cheats to the referee.

b. Referee Rules

  • Referees must observe and call out broken rules.
  • They must coordinate with coaches about strikes.
  • Only illegal moves should be called out.

5. Callouts

These are not rules, but signals used for specific actions or requests. All are legal at any time.

a. Player Callouts

  • Attention – Tap the center top with an open hand to get everyone’s attention.
  • Need Coach – Tap the table with the right hand in “OK” shape.
  • Calling Cheater – Smack table twice and point at cheater. Ref checks footage.
  • Med Vote – Use Attention gesture, then syringe gesture to vote to heal a player (if more than 2 players alive).

b. Coach Callouts

  • Calling cheater (verbal callout to ref).
  • Bell shot (suggestion for saloon).
  • Gold item suggestion (casino).

c. Referee Callouts

  • Strike – Issued for a cheat.
  • No Strike – No cheat detected.
  • Faulty Game – Game must end (disconnect or bug).
